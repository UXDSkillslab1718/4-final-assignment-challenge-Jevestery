UX Design Study Projects

During my studies I already worked on various projects for different courses. These will be - together with short descriptions and insight - listed on this page.

Design & Creativity Portfolio

For the course Design & Creativity I created a portfolio portraying the steps and importance of the design process. The core of this portfolio is to explain and apply the Stanford d.school design process by using its theory in various assignments, such as a design challenge about the topic "How might we spice up waiting time during traveling?".

For a more detailled showcase of this project click here!

Skills Lab: Visual Skills & Programing Bootcamp

The course Skills Lab was split into a part focusing on teaching "Visual Skills" and a "Programing Bootcamp".

Whilst I created a mostly animated visual representation with of website contaning three distinct pages using Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Illustrator and Adobe After Effects for the Visual Skills class, the Programing Bootcamp was my first introduction into the world of coding and the programing language Processing. With Processing I was then able to create responsive programs, such as an RPG-styled video game.

Research for Design Report

A major aspect of the design process is emphatizing with your users, which is done mostly through research. To learn more about the research process the course Research for Design taught about conducting and reporting on research in a proper and professional way. The end project was a research report assessing usability problems users face when using the Rijksstudio Website. By conductions interviews and user trials in a research team I was able to indentify and analyze those usability problems and report on them.
